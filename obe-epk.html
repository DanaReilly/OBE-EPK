Synopsis

OUR BODY ELECTRIC follows three elite women bodybuilders competing to be Ms. Olympia, the most coveted muscle show title. Offering a behind-the-scenes look at women sculpting themselves into anatomical works of art, this feature-length film is both a testament to the power of athletics and an intimate portrait of women defying societal expectations.

Ariel, Amanda, and Chelsea each arrived at bodybuilding by a different road — and each carries a different weight.

Ariel, 33, the youngest IFBB Pro in history, has given her entire adult life to this sport — and she’s not someone who walks away without winning. But the sacrifices are catching up with her, and the life she’s put on hold is starting to feel more urgent than any title.

Amanda, 38, first picked up weights to protect herself from an abusive ex-partner — bodybuilding gave her the power she’d been denied. But in a sport where men’s prize money dwarfs women’s by tenfold, she now wrestles with how much of that power to invest in herself versus the rising career of her boyfriend, Keone.

Chelsea, 35, built her body as an act of defiance — a “revenge physique” forged in response to years of online harassment. To break into the top five and keep her career alive, she must bulk, committing to a year of radical physical transformation that will test her confidence, her brand, and her sense of self.

Their paths converge at the Ms. Olympia — the sport’s most hallowed stage — where all three women must reckon with the sacrifices that brought them there, and ask themselves what comes next.

Shot over a single season with a naturalistic, character-driven visual style, the film immerses audiences in the intimate rhythms of these women’s lives — the pre-dawn training sessions, the precise meal prep, the vulnerability of the pre-competition taper, and the charged silence of standing alone under stage lights.

The Director

Dana Reilly
Director / Producer

Dana Reilly makes documentaries about women on the fringes, navigating the push and pull of patriarchal expectations while carving out identities that are distinctly, stubbornly their own. She is interested in women as active agents — not subjects to be observed, but people in the process of defining themselves.

She is a Professor of Filmmaking at SUNY WCC. Born in New York City, she spent her formative years in London. She holds an MFA in Film Production from the University of Texas at Austin and a BA in International Relations from Brown University.

Director's Statement

Documentary filmmaking is a deeply personal pursuit for me, as I explore women's journeys toward self-actualization while seeking fulfillment through my art.

Like the participants in OUR BODY ELECTRIC, whom I've followed on social media for years, I believe that physical transformation can lead to internal healing. Weightlifting in my thirties transformed my disordered relationship with food and my body; I have found building physical strength to be empowering. To best reflect this subjective perspective and to immerse the audience in the inner worlds of participants, I apply an unadorned, naturalistic visual style to a character-driven film.

I see many connections between the art of bodybuilding and my art of filmmaking. We are all—both bodybuilders and filmmakers—striving to make our fields more accessible and desirable to new talent, reconciling our love for artmaking with the demands of making it profitable, and struggling with the fickle nature of external validation and rewards.

Ariel Khadr is a 3x Arnold Classic Champion. She earned IFBB Pro status at age 17, becoming the youngest IFBB Pro in history, and is the Ms. Fitness International Champion in 2022, 2023, and 2024.

Amanda Kohatsu
@haparican · 106K Followers

Amanda Kohatsu, known online as "Haparican," is a distinguished figure in the fitness community, celebrated for her achievements as a powerlifter, IFBB Professional in Women's Physique Division and Figure, and as an online coach. She has brought her life partner and 3x Olympian Keone Pearson to the Olympia as his coach for two seasons.
